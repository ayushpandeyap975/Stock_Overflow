üìà 1. Short Term Capital Gains (STCG)

Gains from sale of listed equity shares or equity mutual funds held for less than 12 months. Tax rate is 15% (Section 111A) when Securities Transaction Tax (STT) is paid. Losses can be set off against other capital gains and carried forward for up to 8 years.

üìâ 2. Long Term Capital Gains (LTCG)

Gains from listed equity shares/mutual funds held for more than 12 months. Gains up to ‚Çπ1 lakh annually are tax-free; above that taxed at 10% (Section 112A) without indexation benefit. Losses can be set off and carried forward for 8 years.

üîÑ 3. Intraday Trading (Speculative Business Income)

Buying and selling shares on the same day. Considered speculative income under Section 43(5), taxed as per normal slab rates (up to 30%). Business expenses like brokerage, internet, advisory can be claimed.

üìä 4. Futures & Options (F&O Trading)

Classified as non-speculative business income. Taxed as per individual slab rates. Allowed to deduct business expenses. Audit is required if turnover exceeds ‚Çπ10 crores or profit is less than 6% of turnover.

üíµ 5. Dividends

Dividend income from stocks and mutual funds is taxed as per slab rates. If annual dividend exceeds ‚Çπ5,000, companies deduct 10% TDS. No exemption available from FY 2020-21 onwards.

üí∏ 6. Other Income

Includes interest from savings, fixed deposits, rental income, and side business income. Taxed under respective heads at normal slab rates. Must be aggregated for final tax calculation.
